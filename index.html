I am a recent graduate from the second-cycle master's degree in Astrophysics and Cosmology at the University of Bologna. My research focuses on investigating the ISM and dust properties of high-redshift galaxies using JWST observations, with particular emphasis on the Mid-Infrared Instrument (MIRI).

About

Growing up in Austria, I obtained my undergraduate degree in Technical Physics from the Vienna University of Technology (TU Wien) in 2019. Inspired by an Erasmus+ exchange in England, I decided to move abroad and pursue my passion for astronomy through a master's degree at the University of Bologna. Having just finished my studies, I am now ready to take the next step towards a professional career in research through a PhD. You can learn more about me here or download my CV below.

  • CV/Résumé

Research

I study the formation and evolution of massive galaxies at high redshifts, particularly focusing on the dust emission and ISM properties. For my MSc project, I investigated the dust emission properties of 150 galaxies in the COSMOS field I extended the existing photometric catalogue of the Blue Jay survey into the mid-infrared by performing aperture photometry with JWST/MIRI on public data from the PRIMER, COSMOS-Web and COSMOS-3D surveys. Additionally, the measured photometry was used to investigate the assumption of dust energy balance underying many SED fitting codes. More details about my research can be found below.
